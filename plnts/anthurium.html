Anthurium

Description

Anthurium is a genus of herbs often growing as epiphytes on other plants. Some are terrestrial. The leaves are often clustered and are variable in shape. The inflorescence bears small flowers which are perfect, containing male and female structures. The flowers are contained in close together spirals on the spadix.

Beneath the spadix is the spathe, a type of bract. This is variable in shape, as well, but it is lance-shaped in many species. It may extend out flat or in a curve. Sometimes it covers the spadix like a hood. The fruits develop from the flowers on the spadix. They are juicy berries varying in color, usually containing two seeds.

Species

  • Anthurium scherzerianum
  • Anthurium amnicola
  • Anthurium superbum
  • Anthurium hookeri
  • Anthurium radicans
  • Anthurium coriaceum
  • Anthurium veitchii

Care

  1. Use airy and chunky soil.
  2. Place in very bright indirect sunlight.
  3. Water only when the soil dries out almost throughout the pot.
